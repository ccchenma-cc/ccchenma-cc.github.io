The problem behind the automation

Generating a draft is easy. Consistently producing market-appropriate copy that respects product facts, platform conventions, cultural nuance, and regulatory limits requires a coordinated operating system.

Five specialized stages in sequence

Each stage owns one decision and passes structured JSON to the next, making failures traceable and the final output easier to trust.

01

Select the angle

Generate three differentiated marketing angles from the brief and select the strongest route.

02

Generate localized content

Write for the selected platform, format, audience, market, objective, and brand tone.

03

Review compliance

Check unsupported claims, sensitive topics, market risk, and reasons for manual review.

04

Score quality

Evaluate factual accuracy, localization, platform fit, and marketing effectiveness.

05

Rewrite or deliver

Rewrite failed content up to three times, then store approved copy or escalate it to a human.

Localization is a quality dimension, not a translation step

The engine changes pronouns, slang tolerance, cultural references, tone, and platform rules for the United States, Mexico, LATAM, and Spain.

MARKET

Market-aware language

Separate rules for en-US, es-MX, es-419, and es-ES avoid generic Spanish and forced slang.

CHANNEL

Platform-aware output

Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, and Amazon each receive format-specific structure and tone.

SAFETY

Compliance-aware claims

Unsupported absolutes and high-risk topics are revised or routed to human review.

A measurable closed quality loop

Content passes only when the total score reaches 30 and every individual dimension clears its own threshold. Otherwise, feedback returns to the generation stage for another attempt.

Outcome

A working 22-node multi-agent workflow that turns generation into a governed content operation: localized, evaluated, recoverable, and ready for storage in Google Sheets.
